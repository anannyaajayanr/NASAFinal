Why Agriculture

Agriculture, while essential for feeding the world, has a significant negative impact on the environment. Modern farming practices contribute to global warming through deforestation, soil degradation, and the release of greenhouse gases like methane and nitrous oxide from livestock and fertilizers. Agriculture is responsible for approximately 19-29% of global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. Livestock alone contributes 14.5% of total emissions. The use of chemical pesticides and fertilizers further pollutes the air, soil, and water. All these factors combine to make agriculture a leading driver of climate change, creating a pressing need for more sustainable farming practices. Large scale farming also depletes natural resources, leads to biodiversity loss, and consumes enormous amounts of water.

Our Aim

At Roots of Warming, our aim is to raise awareness about the significant impact of modern agriculture on climate change and to foster a dialogue around sustainable practices. We seek to educate our peers and the broader community on how agricultural practices contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and biodiversity loss. Through informative articles, engaging stories, and compelling statistics, we hope to inspire action and encourage individuals to rethink their food choices and advocate for sustainable farming methods. Our goal is not only to highlight the challenges but also to promote solutions that can help mitigate climate change and create a healthier planet for future generations.
